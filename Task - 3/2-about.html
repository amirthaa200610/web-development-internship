How It All Started
2001 – The First Spark
In the heart of Chennai, tucked away in a narrow alley of Mylapore, Gusto Globe took its very first steps. It wasn’t a grand launch or a bustling restaurant — just a tiny rented kitchen, two gas burners, a rickety table, and an unshakable dream. Armed with borrowed utensils and hand-me-down cookware, our founders poured every ounce of passion into their food.

On weekends, a modest food stall emerged outside that kitchen. Amid temple bells and auto horns, a new aroma drifted through — a fusion of Italian herbs, Indian masalas, and Asian sauces. Early dishes like Spiced Lasagna with makhani sauce and Masala Stir-Fry Noodles drew puzzled looks. Fusion cuisine wasn’t common in early 2000s Chennai — it was bold, even risky.

But one bite changed everything. Curious onlookers became loyal customers. That tiny stall quietly transformed into a culinary conversation starter. From the beginning, Gusto Globe was built on courage, creativity, and the belief that food could connect worlds — starting right here in Chennai.
2002 – The Founding Team
With a rising crowd and growing buzz, 2002 marked the formation of our first team of chefs — passionate individuals who believed food could bridge cultures. Some came with classical training, others brought generations of regional Indian cooking wisdom.

Our kitchen became a lab of flavors — naan met herb butter foam, Indian spices danced with balsamic reductions. Late nights were spent testing textures and perfecting plating, all driven by one shared goal: to put fusion on the map in Chennai.

Gusto Globe became more than a stall — it became a movement. A space where culinary magic was born, and every plate told a story far bigger than its ingredients.
So how did the concept of fusion start?

Our journey into fusion cuisine began as an experiment — sparked by curiosity and the desire to create something truly unique. Blending traditional flavors with modern techniques, we wanted every bite to surprise and delight.

“What happens when Hyderabadi royalty meets Italian street charm?”

Back in the early days of Gusto Globe, the team was experimenting with ways to minimize food waste and do something unexpected. One late night in the kitchen, after a long weekend rush, there was a batch of leftover biryani — fragrant, spiced, and too good to throw away.

Someone joked, “What if we rolled it into balls like arancini?” — a dish they’d seen in an old Italian cookbook. That spark of curiosity led to the first bold experiment of its kind: shaping biryani into small spheres, stuffing them with a bit of cheese or spiced paneer, rolling them in crumbs, and deep frying them to a golden crisp.

The result? Magic.

Crunchy on the outside, warm and bursting with biryani flavor inside — every bite was a surprise. It was spicy, savory, rich, and creamy — all at once. It felt nostalgic, yet entirely new.

Guests who tried it thought it was a gourmet creation — little did they know it came from a moment of spontaneity and sustainability. The Biryani Arancini quickly became Gusto Globe’s signature and marked the moment fusion officially took root in our kitchen.

Today, it's not just a dish — it's a symbol of our journey. A reminder that the most extraordinary ideas are sometimes born from the most ordinary leftovers.
